    Section 5 Negation of Statements with Quantifiers

    Objectives: Goals for This Section

    1. Learn universal and existential quantifiers.
      🔗
    2. Learn how to negate statements involving quantifiers.
      🔗
    🔗

    Subsection 5.1 Quantifiers

    Definition 5.1.

    A universal quantifier refers to statements that apply to all elements in a given set. Common words include all, each, every, and no one.
    🔗
    🔗

    Example 5.2. Examples of Universal Quantifiers.

    • All students enjoy learning.
      🔗
    • No one cheats in MATH 103.
      🔗
    🔗

    Definition 5.3.

    An existential quantifier refers to statements that assert the existence of at least one element. Common phrases include some, there exists, and at least one.
    🔗
    🔗

    Example 5.4. Examples of Existential Quantifiers.

    • Some animals live in SC.
      🔗
    • There exists a ghost in my house.
      🔗
    🔗
    🔗

    Subsection 5.2 Negating Statements with Quantifiers

    Definition 5.5.

    The negation of a universal statement becomes an existential statement:
    🔗
    \(\lnot (\text{Universal statement } p) \equiv \text{Existential statement } (\lnot p)\text{.}\)
    🔗
    🔗

    Example 5.6. Negating Universal Statements.

    \(\lnot\)(All students enjoy learning) is equivalent to (Some students do not enjoy learning).
    🔗
    \(\lnot\)(No one cheats in MATH 103) is equivalent to (Some students cheat in MATH 103).
    🔗
    🔗

    Definition 5.7.

    The negation of an existential statement becomes a universal statement:
    🔗
    \(\lnot (\text{Existential statement } p) \equiv \text{Universal statement } (\lnot p)\text{.}\)
    🔗
    🔗

    Example 5.8. Negating Existential Statements.

    \(\lnot\)(Some animals live in SC) is equivalent to (No animals live in SC).
    🔗
    \(\lnot\)(There exists a ghost in my house) is equivalent to (No ghost is in my house).
    🔗
    🔗
    🔗

    Subsection 5.3 Exercises

    Write the negation of each statement.
    🔗

    Question 5.9.

    All animals can swim.
    🔗
    Solution.
    The negation is: Some animals cannot swim.
    🔗
    🔗
    🔗

    Question 5.10.

    Every cat has a tail.
    🔗
    Solution.
    The negation is: Some cats do not have a tail.
    🔗
    🔗
    🔗

    Question 5.11.

    Someone can hear us.
    🔗
    Solution.
    The negation is: No one can hear us.
    🔗
    🔗
    🔗

    Question 5.12.

    There is a man who is three meters tall.
    🔗
    Solution.
    The negation is: No man is three meters tall.
    🔗
    🔗
    🔗
    Write the negation of each compound statement.
    🔗

    Question 5.13.

    Scooby-Doo can swim or jump.
    🔗
    Solution.
    The negation is: Scooby-Doo cannot swim and cannot jump.
    🔗
    🔗
    🔗

    Question 5.14.

    All animals can swim or jump.
    🔗
    Solution.
    The negation is: Some animals cannot swim and cannot jump.
    🔗
    🔗
    🔗

    Question 5.15.

    If Scooby-Doo can swim, then he can jump.
    🔗
    Solution.
    The negation is: Scooby-Doo can swim and he cannot jump.
    🔗
    🔗
    🔗

    Question 5.16.

    If every animal can swim, then every animal can jump.
    🔗
    Solution.
    The negation is: Every animal can swim and some animals cannot jump.
